The project proposal outlines the initial research concept, problem statement, objectives, and proposed methodology for the CoastalAI coastal erosion forecasting framework. This assessment evaluates the feasibility, novelty, and scientific rigor of the proposed approach.

📅 Date: February 2024 🏆 Marks: 12% 📒 Type: Document + Presentation

First progress evaluation covering data acquisition from Copernicus Marine Service, DSAS transect processing, and preliminary feature engineering. Demonstrates completion of the data ingestion pipeline and initial exploratory analysis including monsoon-year alignment and monthly forcing extraction.

📅 Date: June 2024 🏆 Marks: 15% 📒 Type: Presentation + Demo

Second progress evaluation showcasing the ensemble threshold detection implementation (ROC/Youden, Bayesian Logistic, Profile Likelihood, Mutual Information), Mann-Whitney U feature selection results, and Random Forest classification achieving 100% LOO-CV accuracy. Includes bootstrap stability analysis and preliminary SARIMA model fitting.

📅 Date: October 2024 🏆 Marks: 18% 📒 Type: Presentation + Demo

Comprehensive final evaluation of the complete CoastalAI framework including multi-horizon forecasting (H6–H24), per-transect vulnerability mapping across 187 transects, full research paper documentation, and the complete web-based analysis platform. Demonstrates all four research objectives have been achieved.

📅 Date: January 2025 🏆 Marks: 40% 📒 Type: Document + Presentation + Demo

Individual viva voce examination where each team member defends their contribution to the project. Covers theoretical understanding of the ensemble methods, statistical analysis techniques, ML model choices, and the scientific significance of the inverse forcing-erosion relationship discovery.

📅 Date: February 2025 🏆 Marks: 15% 📒 Type: Oral Examination
