Final Document — Component 1: Multi-Source Data Fusion

Data ingestion from DSAS shoreline transects (187 transects) and Copernicus CMEMS NetCDF reanalysis (wave, wind, current). Monsoon-year alignment, temporal aggregation, and 28-feature engineering framework producing a 12×33 analytical dataset.

Available
⬇ Download
📑

Final Document — Component 2: Statistical Feature Selection

Mann–Whitney U testing with Cliff’s delta effect sizes, 5,000-permutation AUC validation. Reduced 28 candidate features to 4 statistically significant erosion drivers (3 with perfect separation |δ| = 1.000). Discovery of the novel inverse forcing-erosion relationship.

Available
⬇ Download
📑

Final Document — Component 3: Ensemble Threshold Detection

Four-method ensemble (ROC/Youden, Bayesian Logistic Regression, Profile Likelihood, Mutual Information) with consensus thresholds (CV < 0.03). 500-tree Random Forest classifier achieving 100% LOO-CV accuracy, validated by 2,000-iteration bootstrap and SHAP interpretability.

Available
⬇ Download
📑

Final Document — Component 4: Multi-Horizon Forecasting & Vulnerability

SARIMA time-series forecasting (ADF stationarity, AIC grid search) across 4 horizons (H6–H24). Monte Carlo uncertainty quantification (2,000 simulations, 95% CI), per-transect vulnerability scoring (187 transects × 4 horizons = 748 risk scores), and KML geospatial output generation.
