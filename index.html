Project Overview

Research Abstract

Coastal erosion is a critical environmental challenge threatening shoreline stability, infrastructure, and coastal ecosystems globally. This study proposes a machine learning framework integrating ensemble threshold detection, Random Forest (RF) classification, and Seasonal Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (SARIMA) time-series forecasting for multi-horizon coastal erosion prediction. The framework ingests multi-source oceanographic datasets — including wave reanalysis, sea surface wind, and ocean current data from the Copernicus Marine Service — alongside Digital Shoreline Analysis System (DSAS) transect measurements spanning 2009–2024. An ensemble of four independent threshold detection methods establishes consensus erosion thresholds with bootstrap stability validation (CV < 0.03). Results reveal that 87.7% of transects experienced net erosion with a mean net shoreline movement of −16.32 m.
Research Components

The Four Core Components

Our framework is organized into four independent research components, each addressing a critical aspect of coastal erosion prediction

📊

Component 1: Multi-Source Data Fusion

A robust pipeline combining DSAS shoreline transects (187 transects, 15 years) with Copernicus Marine Service wave reanalysis (42,601 time steps), sea surface wind, and ocean current data. Includes monsoon-year alignment and feature engineering of 28 environmental variables.

DSAS Copernicus NetCDF xarray
📈

Component 2: Statistical Feature Selection

Rigorous Mann–Whitney U testing with Cliff’s delta effect sizes reduces 28 candidate features to 4 statistically significant drivers (p < 0.05, |δ| ≥ 0.474). Revealed a novel inverse forcing-erosion relationship — all drivers show lower values during erosion years.

Mann-Whitney U Cliff’s δ Permutation
🤖

Component 3: Ensemble Threshold Detection

Four independent methods (ROC/Youden, Bayesian Logistic, Profile Likelihood, Mutual Information) produce consensus erosion thresholds with bootstrap stability (CV < 0.03). A 500-tree Random Forest classifier achieves 100% LOO-CV accuracy.

Random Forest Bootstrap SHAP
🌊

Component 4: Multi-Horizon Forecasting

SARIMA time-series models (AIC grid search, 27 candidates) generate probabilistic erosion forecasts at 6-, 12-, 18-, and 24-month horizons. Monte Carlo uncertainty propagation (2,000 simulations) and per-transect vulnerability mapping across 187 transects.

Novel Discovery

Inverse Forcing-Erosion Relationship

A counterintuitive finding challenging conventional assumptions

💡 Key Discovery

All four statistically significant features exhibited lower values during erosion years, not higher. This challenges the common assumption that coastal erosion always correlates positively with wave energy and storm intensity.

Our analysis reveals that erosion is driven by the absence of protective forcing mechanisms — reduced wave energy available for constructive onshore sediment transport and diminished current-driven sediment supply during erosive periods. Three features showed perfect separation (|δ| = 1.000), meaning every erosion year had lower values than every non-erosion year.
